Olis:
 
  • Main world sources of oils
    • Soja oil. Glycine soja. 26%. USA, Argentina, Brasil and China.
    • Palm oil. Elaeis guineensis Jacq. 18%. Malaysia, Indonèsia, Àfrica.
    • Rape oil. Brassica napus, B. Campestris. 14%. China, India, Canada, EEC.
    • Sunflower oil. Helianthus annuus . 13%. EEC, Russia and Argentina.
  • Six important oils Arachis.
    • Arachis hypogaea. 4.2 mt oil. China (37% of the world crop), India (25%), USA (5%), and various African states (Sudan, Senegal, Nigeria).
    • Cotton. Gossypium hirsutum. 3.7 mt oil. China (26%), USA (18%), India (13%), USSR (12%).
    • Coprah. Cocos nucifera. 2.8 mt oil. Indonesia 30%, Philippines 23% and India 21%.
    • Maize. Zea mays. 1.6 mt oil. Cultivated in temperate regions, this plant is used accessorily for oil production.
    • Olive. (Olea europea. 1.6 mt oil. Spain having the highest crop (32%), Italy (19%), Greece (14%) and Turkey (11%).
    • Sesame. Sesamum indicum. 0.6 mt oil. India (27%), China (21%), Sudan (9%) and Myanmar (Burma) (8%).
  • Minor vegetal oils
    • Grape seed. Vitis vinifera, Vitacae. Temperate regions (mediterranean coasts, USA, Russia, Argentina).
    • Carthame. Carthamus tintorius, Composae. Oriental regions and cultivated in USA .
    • Poppy seed. Papaver somniferum, Papaveracae. Is cultivated in northern and central Europe.
    • Linseed. Linum usitatissimum, Linacae. Is cultivated in temperate regions of Canada, Argentina, India and USA.
    • Almond seed. Prunus amygdalus, Rosacae. Cultivated in temperate regions.
    • Hazel-nut. Corylus avellana, Betulinae. Italy, Spain and Turquey.
    • Walnut. Juglans regia, Juglandacae. Europe and mainly in France.
  • Oils used in dietetics, cosmetics, and lipochemistry
    • Evening primrose. Oenothera biennis, Onagracae.
      • Oil well known for its high content in g-linolenic acid. Numerous varieties are cultivated in America and in Europe. Oil content: 12-30%. The oil is used in pharmacy and cosmetics.
    • Borage. Borago officinalis, Boraginacae.
      • Oil rich in g-linolenic acid (18-25%). Cultivated in west mediterranean regions. Oil content: 13-33%. The oil is used in pharmacy and cosmetic.
    • Black-currant. Ribes nigrum, Saxifragacae. Frequently found in Europe and Asia. The oil, rich in gamma-linolenic acid, is used in oral and parenteral nutrition, cosmetics and pharmacy (Patents by Nestlé SA).
    • Wheat germ. Triticum vulgare, Graminae. The oil is used in dietetic and cosmetic.
    • Cacao. Theobroma cacao, Sterculiacae.
      • The plant originated from Amazonia but is cultivated in Africa and Brazil. Fat content of the roasted kernels: 50- 60%. Cacao butter is used in human nutrition, pharmacy and cosmetics. The world production of cacao beans was about 3 million tons in 1999.
    • Avocado pear (Persea gratissima, Lauracae).
      • The plant originated from America. Oil content of the fruit mesocarp: 5-30% (more in some varieties). The oil is used in pharmacy and cosmetics, it is known to contain a high proportion of unsaponifiable substances (1-2%) with a high percentage of sterols (20-70% of the total), hydrocarbons (5-20%) and triterpen alcohols (6-40%).
    • Castor bean (Ricinus communis, Euphorbiacae).
      • Oil content: about 50%. The oil has a specific fatty acid composition: 1% 18:0, 3% 18:1, 3% 18:2, about 90% hydroxylated 18:0 and 1% dihydroxylated 18:2. After suitable pretreatment, the oil is used in lipochemistry (drying oils, paints), pharmacy and cosmetic. The world production of castor beans was about 1.2 million tons in 1999, India producing about 70% of the total.
    • Kukui oil (Aleurites moluccana, Euphorbiacae).
      • The tree which gives the nuts is the official symbol of the "Aha Kupuna, the Council of Elders of the Nation of Hawaii". The seeds contain a high amount of fluid oil which has many uses, among them lighting (the tree is also named "candlenut tree"), in medicine (purgative, psoriasis, eczema and other dry skin problems), lipochemistry (varnish) and in cosmetics. The oil is said to contain about 30% of linolenic acid and 40% of linoleic acid.
